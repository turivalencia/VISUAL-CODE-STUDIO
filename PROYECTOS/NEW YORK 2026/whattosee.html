Central Park Walking Tour (2-3 hours)

This Central Park walking tour takes you from the south to roughly the center, revealing the most iconic spots in 2 to 3 hours.(for a longer walk see navigation menu)
Enter Central Park from the southeast corner and stroll along The Pond until you reach the picturesque Gapstow Bridge. As a backdrop, you'll have fantastic views of the Billionaires' Row skyscrapers and the Plaza Hotel, which contrast beautifully with the small bridge surrounded by greenery.
Next stop is the famous ice rink The Wollman Rink. Soon we'll arrive at the famous tree-lined Mall, one of Central Park's iconic landmarks. One of the most important sculptures on the Mall is the Suffragette Statue which depicts three women's rights activists: Susan B. Anthony, Sojourner Truth, and Elizabeth Cady Stanton. Follow the Mall to the end to find Bethesda Terrace. From there, you can admire the magnificent Bethesda Fountain, Central Park's most famous fountain, from above.
At this point, we follow the Bethesda Walk, a path that runs horizontally through the park, passing by some beautiful spots. the lake At the Central Park Boathouse we can rent a rowboat to sail on Central Park's lake. Continue walking east, and you'll reach one great spots in Central Park, the circular Conservatory Water pond. Just north of the pond, a surprise awaits: the statue of Alice in Wonderland, dedicated to Lewis Carroll's book. If we look closely, on the pond's west bank we'll also see a sculpture dedicated to the writer Hans Christian Andersen.
Retrace your steps back to Bethesda Fountain, but this time, follow the lake shore westward until you reach Bow Bridge. You've surely seen this romantic bridge in countless movies. Walk along the lake to the south and follow the signs to Strawberry Fields, the John Lennon memorial in Central Park. It's located near the Dakota building, where the musician was murdered, and is named after one of his songs ("Strawberry Fields Forever"). Strawberry Fields is a meadow, but it features a famous mosaic with the word "Imagine" on the ground, where musicians often play.
This walking tour of Central Park ends here! We can exit to the west and, if we have more time, stroll along the Upper West Side, visit Lincoln Center… Or, if you prefer, the famous American Museum of Natural History is just a few blocks away.
