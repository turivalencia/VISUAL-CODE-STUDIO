TRANSPORT

Getting Around NYC Best ways to travel around the City with are subways, buses, ferries, biking and of course walking.

MTA: Subways and Buses

The MTA uses a contactless payment system called OMNY (One Metro New York) for all buses, subway trains and Roosevelt Island Tram stations. You can buy and reload a dedicated OMNY card for $5. A single trip costs $3 and yo can use the same card or device to get a free transfer within two hours of first use. Intereint to take a seven days ticket because after you spend $35 in fares ( 12 trips) within a seven-day period from your first tap, you receive free, unlimited rides for the rest of that period. Single-ride OMNY tickets are also available for $3.50.

Ferries

NYC Ferry is an extensive waterway transportation network, with multiple stops in Manhattan, Brooklyn and Queens; a couple in the Bronx (Soundview and Throgs Neck) and one in Staten Island; as well as Roosevelt Island, Governors Island and Rockaway Beach. You can take a boat ride on any route for $4.50 and a 10-trip pass is $29. Transfers are free within two hours of your first trip. A 10-trip pass is $29.
